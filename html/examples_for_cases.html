Cases Explanation

Nominative: The thing that does the verb.

The man walked to the forum.

Genitive: Used to mark possession.

I took the baker's offer.

Dative: The indirect object, translated with "for".

It worked out for my friend.

Pointerial: The indirect object, translated with "to".

A send a letter to the mother.

Accusative: The thing that has the verb done to it.

I ate the cookie.

Ablative: The thing which prepositions refer to.

I am walking to the city.
